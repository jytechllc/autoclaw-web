# AutoClaw GEO Playbook

## What GEO Means Here

GEO means `Generative Engine Optimization`.

The goal is not only to rank in classic search, but also to make AutoClaw easier to cite in:

- ChatGPT answers
- Perplexity answers
- Google AI Overviews
- answer-style search results

## What Answer Engines Tend To Prefer

- direct answers to specific buyer questions
- pages with stable facts and simple headings
- examples, workflows, comparisons, and definitions
- content that can be quoted in short blocks without extra context

## Lowest-Cost GEO Tactics For AutoClaw

### 1. Publish narrow answer pages

Do not write generic brand pages only.

Good examples:

- how to build outbound pipeline for the US market
- what a sample lead pack should include
- when a B2B team needs contact enrichment
- how much outbound can be automated before hiring SDRs

### 2. Structure pages for extraction

Use headings that match buyer questions:

- what problem does this solve
- who is it for
- what happens in the first 14 days
- what metrics should be tracked

### 3. Keep claims concrete

Prefer:

- number of contacts enriched
- time to first outbound launch
- who the workflow fits

Avoid:

- vague claims about revolutionizing marketing
- generic "all-in-one AI" positioning

### 4. Reuse the same factual language across channels

The same core claims should appear in:

- homepage
- use-case pages
- outreach emails
- social posts
- sample lead packs

That consistency improves citation quality and buyer trust.

## Weekly GEO Work

Every week:

1. publish or update one public answer-style page
2. turn one outbound objection into a short public FAQ
3. turn one customer workflow into a case-style post
4. update internal language if replies show mismatch between promise and market need

## GEO Success Metrics

- impressions and clicks from search
- branded search growth
- referral visits from answer engines when measurable
- calls booked from educational pages
- language reused by prospects in replies or calls
